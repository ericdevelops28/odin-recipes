Shrimp Ceviche

What is Ceviche?

Originating from the coastal sides of Peru, it's become a staple along the coasts of South America, Mexico and beyond. Ceviche is a dish that is usually served as an appetizer. It's generally made from raw fresh fish or shrimp, that's marinated in lemon and/or lime citrus juices. The acidity in the citrus cures the fish and essentially cooks it. Usually after your seafood of choice has been cooked, the rest of the fresh ingredients are usually added once ready.

Ceviche is a perfect summer dish, due to it's fresh assortment of ingredients during the hot summer days. It's truly a vibrant dish that catches the eye, whether as an appetizer or a main dish to sit down and enjoy! This type of dish is easy and simple to make and is great for a weekend or parties! It's truly a dish seafood lovers will instantly fall in love with, making it a staple for the summer! Pair it up with some fresh tortilla chips, and nice cool summer drink and you are set to go!

This version of ceviche will be made using shrimp as our seafood of choice. There's no one way to make ceviche, there are endless options of seafood to substitute, and it all comes down to preference! Each country, city, restaurant and household has their own way of making it. At it's core, this recipe includes jalapeños and avocados, making it a Mexican Ceviche dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of peeled and deveined raw medium shrimp
  • 1 tsp of sea salt
  • 1 tsp of black pepper
  • 3 - 5 limes
  • 1 medium avocado diced
  • 1 medium jalapeños, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 red onion finely diced
  • 10-12 small cherry tomatoes cut up in bite size pieces
  • Tortilla chips, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Place your deveined shrimp into a medium sized bowl or larger if you prefer.
  2. Add your 3-5 fresh limes and squeeze them, over your shrimp.
  3. Once you've added the lemon juice, completely cover the shrimp and refrigerate for 30 minutes or until the shrimp look opaque and appear slightly firm.
  4. After 30 minutes have passed, add your fresh cilantro, jalapeño, red onion and your cherry tomatoes.
  5. Toss and combine all of the ingredients.
  6. Sprinkle in your salt and pepper to your desired taste.
  7. Once all the ingredients have mixed, cover and refrigerate for 1 hour to let it rest.
  8. Before serving add your diced avocados, and mix gently.
  9. Serve with a side of corn chips (optional) and your all finished!
